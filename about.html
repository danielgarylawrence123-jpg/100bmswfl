⬥ Who We Are

About Us

100 Black Men of Southwest Florida is a proud local chapter of 100 Black Men of America, Inc. — a leading organization of African American men committed to the intellectual development of youth and the economic empowerment of the African American community. Through our Four Pillars of Mentoring, Education, Health & Wellness, and Economic Empowerment, we develop leaders who transform Southwest Florida.

⬥ Our Story

Built by men who
stayed for the work.

1963

The National Vision

A group of influential African American leaders in New York conceive the idea for 100 Black Men of America. Early members include David Dinkins, J. Bruce Llewellyn, and Jackie Robinson.

1986

National Incorporation

100 Black Men of America, Inc. is formally established as chapters spread nationwide. The "Four for the Future" framework becomes the standard for all chapters.

SWFL Chapter Founded

Southwest Florida Gets Its Chapter

A group of committed men in Fort Myers charter the Southwest Florida chapter, bringing the national mission to Lee, Collier, and Charlotte counties. Bridge Builders launches at Fort Myers Middle Academy.

Exact founding year pending board verification.

Today

500+ Youth Mentored and Growing

Six active programs, the annual Drew Gala, SWFL's premier Juneteenth celebration, and expanding partnerships with the Boys and Girls Club, D1 Gym, and community organizations. Over $100,000 in scholarships awarded.
